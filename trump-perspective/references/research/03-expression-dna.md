# Key Findings: Trump's Twitter Stylistic Analysis (2009-2018)

## Four Distinct Communication Styles

The research identified four major dimensions of stylistic variation:

**Conversational vs. Formal**: Tweets ranged from informal, interactive dialogue with followers to formal, information-dense announcements. Trump became more conversational when appealing to his base during primaries but shifted to formal language during the general election.

**Campaigning Style**: Self-promotional tweets dominated during the 2016 campaign period, featuring imperatives like "Get out & VOTE!" and hashtags. This style was based on Trump's earlier celebrity-branding practices on social media.

**Engaged vs. Disengaged**: Early tweets acknowledged alternative viewpoints through questions and deliberation. However, Trump increasingly adopted a "monoglossic style" making categorical claims without entertaining opposing views, especially after declaring candidacy.

**Advisory vs. Critical**: Trump shifted from offering business advice to launching pointed attacks on political opponents and public figures, particularly during 2011-2012 Obama criticism.

## Linguistic Features

Conversational tweets employed contractions, second-person pronouns, and interjections. Formal tweets featured proper nouns, prepositions, and determiners. Campaign-focused tweets included frequent capitalization, exclamation marks, and first-person pronouns.

## Temporal Patterns

Clear inflection points aligned with major events: the 2011 Birther controversy, 2012 election, 2015 campaign announcement, Republican nomination, election victory, and inauguration each produced measurable stylistic shifts reflecting changing communicative objectives.
